Sea Egg Facts You Didn't Know

They lose their spines - After death, the spines detach and fall off, leaving behind just the smooth, round test (skeleton).

Their skeletons bleach white - The colorful exterior fades away, and dead sea urchin tests become pale white or cream-colored when washed up on beaches.

They're popular collectibles - Beachcombers often collect the hollow, delicate tests as souvenirs because of their intricate geometric patterns.

They decompose quickly - The soft tissue inside decomposes rapidly, but the calcium carbonate test can persist for years if conditions are right.
